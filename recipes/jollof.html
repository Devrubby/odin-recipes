Jollof Rice

Description

Jollof rice is a rice dish, that is so popurlar and prevalent in West African.

It is a food variety that is well recognised around the whole of West Africa region

On this page, I will be showing you how to prepare the popular Nigeria jollof rice

It is always a hot debate between Nigerians and Ghanians on which variety of Jollof rice that is more delicious

Ingredients

  • 750g long grain parboiled rice
  • 3-4 cups of tin tomato
  • Pepper and salt
  • 2 medium sized onions
  • 3 knorr and maggi cubes
  • 2 teasspoons of thyme
  • 2 teasspoons of curry powder
  • Chicken

Steps

  1. Wash the rice in cold water (optional) and place in a pot.
  2. Add some water, about 4 times the level of the rice
  3. Set on the stove and leave to cook
  4. Note when the water starts boiling and leave to cook till the rice is rubbery.
    One way to check this is to bite into a grain of the rice being parboiled.
    If the rice is hard to the bite or even makes a sound when you bite, it is not yet OK to rinse it.
  5. Pour the contents of the pot in a sieve.
  6. Cool down the rice rapidly by dipping the sieve in cold water, changing water often till the rice is cold.
  7. Leave in the sieve to drain off all the water.
  8. The rice is now ready to be cooked again.
  9. Pour the chicken stock and the tomato stew into a sizeable pot and leave to boil.
  10. Add the final product of step 7, curry powder, salt and pepper to taste
  11. Cover the pot and leave to cook on low to medium heat. This way the rice does not burn before the water dries up.
